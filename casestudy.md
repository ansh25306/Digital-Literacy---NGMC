## Case Study: Phishing

Phishing is a type of cybercrime where attackers try to trick individuals into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or personal data.
It usually happens through fake emails, messages, or websites that appear to be from trusted sources like banks or companies.
In a typical phishing attack, the attacker sends an email that looks official and asks the user to click on a link.
This link redirects to a fake website that looks identical to the original. When the user enters their login details, the attacker captures the information.
Students and working professionals are common targets because they frequently use emails and online services. 
The consequences of phishing include financial loss, identity theft, and unauthorized access to accounts.
Phishing attacks are increasing rapidly, making it important for users to stay alert and verify all online communications before taking action.
